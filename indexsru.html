Development and Performance Testing of SPV Powered Mobile Canteen

The SPV (Solar Photovoltaic) powered mobile canteen is a food van that uses solar energy instead of fuel to run its operations. Solar panels are installed on the roof of the canteen to capture sunlight and convert it into electricity. This energy is stored in batteries and used to power lights, fans, and small kitchen appliances like water heaters and cookers. The system is tested to check how much energy it can produce, how long the battery lasts, and how well it performs in different weather conditions. It is eco-friendly, reduces the use of petrol or diesel, and lowers pollution. This type of mobile canteen is especially useful in remote areas or during emergencies where electricity is not easily available. It also helps save money on fuel and promotes the use of renewable energy.

Optimal Sizing of Grid Connected AC Irrigation Pumps for Energy Conservation

The optimal sizing of grid-connected AC irrigation pumps is focused on selecting the correct size of pumps to ensure energy efficiency in agricultural irrigation. These pumps are powered by electricity from the grid and are used to provide water for crops. If the pump is oversized, it consumes more energy than necessary, leading to wasted electricity and higher costs. On the other hand, if the pump is undersized, it may not provide enough water, resulting in poor crop growth. By carefully calculating the right size of the pump based on the farm's size, crop type, and water requirements, farmers can ensure efficient use of energy. This approach helps conserve electricity, reduce operational costs, and make farming practices more sustainable, benefiting both the farmer and the environment.
